What is JASS?

JASS is a css framework that useful to make reponsive and customizable website, easy to use and user-friendly, just copy and paste that's it.

v1.0.0 JASS (old version)
Introduction
Get started with JASS,a css framework for building responsive, mobile-first sites.
Quick start
Looking to quickly add JASS to your project? follow the steps shown below.
CSS
Copy-paste the stylesheet <link> into your <head> before all other stylesheets to load our CSS.
<link href=" https://jainilsoni1706.github.io/newJASS/v3CSS.css " rel=" stylesheet " >
JavaScript
Many of our components require the use of JavaScript to function. Copy-paste the <script> into your either <head> or <body>tag
<script src=" https://jainilsoni1706.github.io/newJASS/v3JS.js " > </script>
HTML5 Doctype
JASS requires the use of the HTML5 doctype. Without it, you’ll see some funky incomplete styling, but including it shouldn’t cause any considerable hiccups.
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>

  . . . . .

</html>
Responsive meta tag
JASS is developed mobile first, a strategy in which we optimize code for mobile devices first and then scale up components as necessary using CSS media queries. To ensure proper rendering and touch zooming for all devices, add the responsive viewport meta tag to your <head>.
<meta name=" viewport " content=" width=device-width, initial-scale=1 " >

Helix Nebula

The Helix Nebula (also known as NGC 7293 or Caldwell 63) is a planetary nebula (PN) located in the constellation Aquarius. Discovered by Karl Ludwig Harding, probably before 1824, this object is one of the closest to the Earth of all the bright planetary nebulae.[4] The distance, measured by the Gaia mission, is 655±13 light-years.[5] It is similar in appearance to the Cat's Eye Nebula and the Ring Nebula, whose size, age, and physical characteristics are similar to the Dumbbell Nebula, varying only in its relative proximity and the appearance from the equatorial viewing angle.[6] The Helix Nebula has sometimes been referred to as the "Eye of God" in pop culture,[7] as well as the "Eye of Sauron".
